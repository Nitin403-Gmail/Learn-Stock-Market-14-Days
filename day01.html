üìà What Is The Stock Market, Really?

Forget the complicated explanations. At its core, the stock market is simply a marketplace where people buy and sell ownership pieces (shares) of companies.

The Simple Truth

When you buy a stock, you're buying a tiny piece of that company. If the company does well and grows, your piece becomes more valuable. If it struggles, your piece loses value.

üí° Key Insight

Stock prices move for one reason only: supply and demand. If more people want to buy than sell, prices go up. If more people want to sell than buy, prices go down. Everything else‚Äîearnings reports, news, technical patterns‚Äîonly matters because they influence buying and selling decisions.

The Main Players

  • Retail Traders (You) - Individual investors trading their own money
  • Institutional Investors - Big funds, banks, pension funds with massive capital
  • Market Makers - Firms that provide liquidity by always offering to buy and sell
  • Exchanges - NYSE, NASDAQ - the venues where trades happen

How Trades Actually Happen

  1. You place an order to buy 10 shares of Apple at $175
  2. Your broker sends this order to an exchange or market maker
  3. The system matches your buy order with someone willing to sell at $175
  4. The trade executes, and you now own those shares

This happens in milliseconds. The complexity behind the scenes doesn't change what you need to focus on: buying low and selling high.

üß† The 80/20 Principle in Trading

"The majority of profits come from a minority of trades. The majority of losses come from fighting this truth." ‚Äî Trading Wisdom

The Pareto Principle states that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes. In trading, this manifests in several critical ways:

How 80/20 Applies to Your Trading

  • 20% of your trades will generate 80% of your profits
  • 20% of trading concepts will create 80% of your edge
  • 20% of stocks will provide 80% of your opportunities
  • 20% of your time should be trading; 80% should be waiting

‚è∞ Choose Your Style: Trading vs Investing

Before going further, you need to decide what approach suits your personality and lifestyle:

Day Trading

  • Buying and selling within the same day
  • Requires 2-4+ hours of screen time daily
  • Higher stress, faster profits (or losses)
  • Needs $25,000+ for US pattern day trading rules

Swing Trading (Recommended for Most)

  • Holding positions for days to weeks
  • Requires 30-60 minutes daily
  • Balanced risk/reward profile
  • Works with any account size

Position Trading / Investing

  • Holding for months to years
  • Requires a few hours weekly
  • Lower stress, benefits from compounding
  • Focuses more on fundamentals

üí° Recommendation

This course focuses primarily on swing trading principles because they offer the best risk/reward for most people. However, the concepts apply to all timeframes‚Äîthe same support/resistance level that matters on a 15-minute chart matters on a weekly chart.
