üìî Why You Must Keep a Trading Journal

"The definition of insanity is doing the same thing over and over and expecting different results." ‚Äî Often attributed to Einstein

Most traders make the same mistakes repeatedly because they don't track what they're doing. A trading journal changes everything.

Benefits of a Trading Journal

  • Identifies patterns: You'll see which setups work and which don't
  • Exposes emotional trading: Hard to deny patterns when data shows them
  • Accelerates learning: Review mistakes without having to experience them again
  • Builds confidence: See your actual edge in the data
  • Keeps you accountable: Forces honesty about your trading

üí° The 10x Learning Multiplier

A trader who keeps a journal and reviews it weekly will improve 10x faster than one who doesn't. Why? Because you learn from EVERY trade‚Äîwins, losses, and mistakes‚Äîinstead of just the ones you remember.

üìä Key Performance Metrics to Track

1. Win Rate

Formula: (Winning Trades √∑ Total Trades) √ó 100

Target: 40-60% is good. Higher isn't always better if R:R is low.

2. Average R (Risk Multiple)

Formula: Average profit/loss expressed as multiples of risk

Example: If you risk $100 and average $150 profit, your average R is 1.5R

Target: Positive average R means you're profitable

3. Expectancy

Formula: (Win Rate √ó Avg Win) - (Loss Rate √ó Avg Loss)

What it means: Expected $ per trade over time

Target: Positive expectancy = profitable system

4. Maximum Drawdown

What it is: Largest peak-to-trough decline in account value

Why it matters: Shows your risk exposure

Target: Keep under 20% if possible

5. Profit Factor

Formula: Gross Profits √∑ Gross Losses

What it means: How much you make for every $ you lose

Target: Above 1.5 is good, above 2.0 is excellent

üìÖ Weekly Review Process

Set aside 30-60 minutes every weekend for your trading review:

Step 1: Gather the Data (10 min)

  • Calculate your week's statistics (wins, losses, R total)
  • Note any pattern in your results

Step 2: Review Each Trade (20 min)

  • Look at screenshots‚Äîdid setups match your criteria?
  • Check emotional notes‚Äîany patterns?
  • Did you follow your rules? Grade each trade A-F

Step 3: Identify Patterns (15 min)

  • Which setups performed best?
  • Which days/times were most profitable?
  • What mistakes keep repeating?
  • Are you breaking any trading rules consistently?

Step 4: Create Action Items (10 min)

  • One thing to STOP doing
  • One thing to START doing
  • One thing to CONTINUE doing

üí° The Compound Effect

If you improve just 1% each week through journaling and review, you'll be 67% better in one year. Small, consistent improvements compound dramatically over time.
