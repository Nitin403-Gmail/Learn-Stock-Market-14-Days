Dividend vs. Growth vs. Value: Finding Your Style

‚è± 50 minutes üìö Investment Styles üéØ Personality Fit

üéØ Today's Learning Objectives

  1. Understand the three core pillars of investing styles.
  2. Learn the power of "Yield on Cost" in Dividend Growth Investing (DGI).
  3. Identify the characteristics of a true Growth investment.
  4. Contrast "Deep Value" with high-quality compounders (Quality Investing).
  5. Self-assess which style matches your goals and temperament.

üìä 1. Dividend Growth Investing (DGI)

Focuses on companies that not only pay a dividend but increase it every single year. These are often "Dividend Aristocrats."

üí° Yield on Cost

If you buy a stock at $100 paying a $3 dividend (3% yield), and they grow that dividend by 10% every year, in 10 years they will be paying $7.78. Your "yield on your original cost" is now 7.78%. In 20 years, it could be over 20%! This is how patient investors live off their portfolios.

Ideal for: Those seeking passive income and stability.

üöÄ 2. Growth Investing

Focuses on companies increasing their revenue and earnings at a faster rate than the average market. Dividends are rare because they reinvest all cash to grow faster.

  • Pros: Potential for "multi-bagger" returns (10x, 20x).
  • Cons: High volatility and high valuation (expensive P/E). If the growth slows down, the price can crash 50% quickly.

Ideal for: Younger investors with long timelines and high risk tolerance.

üíé 3. Value & Quality Investing

Deep Value: Looking for "ugly" stocks at extremely low valuations. Buying a dollar's worth of assets for 50 cents. (The Ben Graham style).

Quality (GARP): Growth At A Reasonable Price. Looking for wonderful businesses with high moats (Day 6) and high ROE (Day 4) and buying them when their price is "fair." (The Warren Buffett style).

üåü Which is better?

Statistically, high-quality compounders (Quality) tend to outperform over decades with less stress than "Deep Value" cigar butts.

‚úÖ Today's Action Steps

üìù Action Step 1: Temperament Check (10 minutes)

How would you feel if your portfolio dropped 30% in a month, even if the businesses were fine? If you would panic, you are likely a Dividend or Value investor. If you would see it as a "buying opportunity," you might be suited for Growth.

üìù Action Step 2: Spot the Style (20 minutes)

Identify one stock for each category:

  1. A Dividend King (50+ years of increases).
  2. A Hyper-Growth tech stock.
  3. A Quality Compounder (High ROE, fair P/E).

üîë Key Takeaways

  1. DGI is the ultimate strategy for building a growing income stream.
  2. Growth stocks require the most emotional discipline to hold through crashes.
  3. Quality investing is the most reliable path for most individual investors.
  4. The best style is the one you can actually stick to when the market gets ugly.
