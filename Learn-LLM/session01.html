What are Large Language Models?

ü§ñ Definition

Large Language Models (LLMs) are AI systems trained on massive amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like text. They can perform a wide variety of language tasks including:

  • Writing articles and stories
  • Answering questions
  • Translating languages
  • Writing code
  • Analyzing documents
Traditional AI vs LLMs
Traditional AI Large Language Models
Narrow, task-specific General-purpose language understanding

How Do Large Language Models Work?

üß† The Transformer Architecture

LLMs are built on the Transformer architecture, introduced in a groundbreaking 2017 paper by Google researchers. This architecture revolutionized AI by enabling models to:

  • Process text in parallel (much faster training)
  • Understand context from entire sentences
  • Generate coherent responses based on patterns
  • Handle long-range dependencies in text
