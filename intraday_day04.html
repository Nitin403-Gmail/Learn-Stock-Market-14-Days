Most beginners memorize "Hammer," "Doji," "Harami." This is useless if you don't understand WHY the candle formed.

üí° The Mentor's Secret

"A candlestick is not a shape. It is a war story between Buyers (Bulls) and Sellers (Bears) over a specific time period. Your job is not to name the candle, but to see who won the war."

üîé Anatomy of the War (OHLC)

Every candle tells you four things:

  • Open: Who was in control when the bell rang?
  • High: How far did the Bulls push?
  • Low: How far did the Bears push?
  • Close: Who had the final say? (The most important price).

The Wick (Tail): A long wick represents Rejection. If you see a long wick at the bottom, it means Bears tried to push it down, but Bulls pushed them back. That is hidden buying pressure.

üî• The 3 Power Candles You MUST Know

Forget the 50 exotic patterns. In intraday trading, these 3 tell you 90% of the story.

‚ñÆ

1. The Power Candle (Marubozu/Elephant)

Appearance: Big body, tiny or no wicks.
Story: Complete dominance. If Green, buyers are aggressive. If Red, sellers are panicking.
Action: Don't trade against this candle. The trend is strong.

‚úö

2. The Indecision Candle (Doji/Spinning Top)

Appearance: Tiny body, long wicks on both sides.
Story: War is tied. Buyers and sellers are equally matched. Confusion.
Action: Wait. A big move is usually coming next. Do not enter inside a Doji.

üî®

3. The Rejection Candle (Hammer/Pinbar)

Appearance: Small body on one end, very long tail on the other.
Story: A trap. One side tried to push price, but got brutally rejected.
Action: A clear reversal signal. If found at Support (Hammer) or Resistance (Shooting Star), it is a high-probability trade.

üìç Context is King

A "Hammer" candle in the middle of nowhere means NOTHING. Location is 50% of the trade.

  • Hammer at Support: Bullish Signal (Buy) ‚úÖ
  • Hammer at Resistance: Weird/Ignore ‚ùå
  • Shooting Star at Resistance: Bearish Signal (Sell) ‚úÖ
