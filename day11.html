üéØ Today's Learning Objectives

  2. Learn the 5 key metrics that professional investors use

  4. Combine fundamentals with technicals for better trades

üìä When Do Fundamentals Matter?

"In the short run, the market is a voting machine. In the long run, it's a weighing machine." ‚Äî Benjamin Graham

Fundamentals Matter More For:

  • Long-term investors (holding months to years)
  • Position traders (holding weeks to months)
  • Stock selection (choosing which stocks to trade)
  • Avoiding value traps (companies that look cheap but are failing)

Fundamentals Matter Less For:

  • Day traders (price action dominates)
  • Short-term swing traders (technical setups drive entries)
  • Index/ETF traders (trading the market, not individual companies)

üí° The 80/20 of Fundamentals

You don't need to be a financial analyst. For most traders, understanding 5 key metrics is enough to avoid bad stocks and identify quality companies. Leave the deep financial modeling to the hedge funds.

üìà The 5 Key Metrics

1. P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)

What it is: Stock price divided by earnings per share (EPS)

What it tells you: How much you're paying for each dollar of earnings

How to use it:

  • Low P/E (< 15): Potentially undervalued or slow growth
  • High P/E (> 25): High growth expectations or overvalued
  • Compare to industry peers, not just absolute numbers

Example: Stock at $100 with EPS of $5 = P/E of 20 (you pay $20 for every $1 of earnings)

2. Revenue Growth

What it is: Year-over-year percentage change in total sales

What it tells you: Is the company growing its business?

How to use it:

  • Growing revenue = healthy, expanding business
  • Declining revenue = potential problems ahead
  • Look for consistent growth, not one-time spikes

Good sign: 10%+ year-over-year revenue growth

3. EPS Growth (Earnings Per Share)

What it is: Year-over-year percentage change in earnings per share

What it tells you: Is the company becoming more profitable?

How to use it:

  • Growing EPS = increasing profitability
  • EPS growth > Revenue growth = improving margins
  • Negative or declining EPS = caution

Good sign: 15%+ year-over-year EPS growth

4. Debt-to-Equity Ratio

What it is: Total debt divided by shareholders' equity

What it tells you: How much the company relies on debt

How to use it:

  • Low D/E (< 0.5): Conservative, financially stable
  • High D/E (> 2): Risky, vulnerable in downturns
  • Varies by industry (banks have high D/E by nature)

Good sign: D/E under 1.0 for most industries

5. ROE (Return on Equity)

What it is: Net income divided by shareholders' equity

What it tells you: How efficiently the company uses investor capital

How to use it:

  • High ROE (> 15%): Efficient capital use
  • Low ROE (< 10%): Poor capital efficiency
  • Consistent ROE is better than volatile

Good sign: ROE above 15% consistently

üîç Quick Fundamental Screening

Use this checklist to quickly assess if a stock is worth trading:

Metric What You Want Red Flag
P/E Ratio Reasonable for industry Extremely high (>50) or negative
Revenue Growth Positive, ideally >10% Declining for 2+ quarters
EPS Growth Positive, ideally >15% Negative or declining
Debt/Equity < 1.0 > 2.0 (outside finance sector)
ROE > 15% < 10% or negative

‚ö° Combining Fundamentals with Technicals

The most powerful approach combines both:

  1. Use fundamentals to SELECT stocks: Screen for quality companies with good metrics
  2. Use technicals to TIME entries: Wait for pullbacks, support levels, confirmation patterns
  3. Use technicals to MANAGE trades: Stop-losses, profit targets, trailing stops

Example Process:

  1. Screen for stocks with: P/E < 25, Revenue growth > 10%, ROE > 15%
  2. Check which of these are in uptrends (above 200 EMA)
  3. Wait for pullback to support or 50 EMA
  4. Enter on bullish candlestick confirmation
  5. Manage with technical stop and target

üí° The Bottom Line

Trade quality companies (good fundamentals) at good prices (technical setups). This combination gives you an edge in both the what (stock selection) and the when (timing).

üîë Key Takeaways
