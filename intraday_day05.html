Imagine price is a bouncing ball in a multi-story building. It hits the floor and bounces up. It hits the ceiling and falls down.

SUPPORT (Demand Zone) - Buyers Waiting
  • Support (Demand Zone): A price level where buyers are strong enough to prevent the price from falling further. "The Floor".
  • Resistance (Supply Zone): A price level where selling is strong enough to prevent the price from rising further. "The Ceiling".

‚úçÔ∏è Drawing Zones Like a Pro

Beginners draw thin lines. Professionals draw rectangular zones. Why? Because price is not perfect. It can overshoot by 50 cents and reverse.

Rule 1: Look Left

History repeats itself. Look to the left of the chart. Where did price reverse sharply before? That is a zone.

Rule 2: Touch Points

A valid zone needs at least 2-3 touches. The more times price touches and reverses, the stronger the zone is.

Rule 3: Use Bodies, Not Just Wicks

Draw your zone covering the candle bodies and the wicks of the reversal area to capture the noise.

üîÑ The Concept of Role Reversal/Polarity

This is where the magic happens. When a ball breaks through a ceiling, that ceiling becomes the new floor.

  • If Resistance breaks, it becomes Support.
  • If Support breaks, it becomes Resistance.

üí° Mentor's Strategy

Don't just chase a breakout. Wait for the Retest.
Example: Price breaks Resistance ‚Üí Wait for it to come back down to the old Resistance (now Support) ‚Üí Look for a Green Candle ‚Üí BUY.
