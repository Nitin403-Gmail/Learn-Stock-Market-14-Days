Indicators are Filters, Not Drivers

Price Action (Day 4 & 5) is the driver. Indicators are the GPS. They can tell you the speed and direction, but they cannot drive the car. Never take a trade solely because an indicator crossed over. Use them to confirm what Price Action is already telling you.

1. VWAP (The Institutional Benchmark)

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) Intraday Only

VWAP shows the average price a stock has traded at throughout the day, based on both volume and price. Institutions use it to check if they are getting a good price.

  • Above VWAP: Bulls are in control. Only look for Buy trades. ‚úÖ
  • Below VWAP: Bears are in control. Only look for Sell trades. üîª
  • Concept: Think of VWAP as a magnet. If price goes too far away, it snaps back. If it stays close, it might breakout.

2. RSI (Momentum Gauge)

RSI (Relative Strength Index) Momentum

Measures how fast price is moving. It ranges from 0 to 100.

  • Overbought (>70): Price might be exhausted. Don't buy here. (Does NOT mean Sell immediately).
  • Oversold (<30):< /strong> Price might be cheap. Don't short here.
  • Divergence: The Secret Weapon. If Price makes a Higher High, but RSI makes a Lower High, the trend is dying. Reversal is coming!

3. 20 EMA (The Trend Wave)

20 EMA (Exponential Moving Average) Trend Following

The average price of the last 20 candles. It acts as dynamic support/resistance.

  • Price surfing above 20 EMA: Strong Uptrend. Buy the dips to the line.
  • Price surfing below 20 EMA: Strong Downtrend. Sell the rallies to the line.
  • Flat Line: Market is Sideways. Do nothing.
