üåç Understanding Market Nature

Most beginners think the market is random chaos. It is not. The market is a mechanism for price discovery, and it moves in cycles. Understanding these cycles is like having a map in a dense forest.

If you buy when the market is preparing to drop (Distribution), you will lose. If you sell when the market is preparing to rise (Accumulation), you will lose. Today, we learn to read the map.

üîÑ The 4 Phases of the Market

Every stock, index, or crypto moves through these four phases repeatedly on all timeframes (1-minute, 15-minute, Daily, Weekly).

1

Accumulation Phase (The Base)

This happens after a downtrend. Prices flatten out. Smart money (Institutions) starts buying quietly. The chart looks like it's moving sideways.

2

Advancement Phase (The Uptrend)

Price breaks out of the Accumulation zone. This is where we want to be buying. The trend is clearly Up. Higher Highs and Higher Lows.

3

Distribution Phase (The Top)

After a good run, the trend stalls. Smart money starts selling their shares to late retail buyers. Price moves sideways again but looks "heavy".

4

Declining Phase (The Downtrend)

Support breaks. Panic sets in. Price falls rapidly. We engage in Short Selling here to profit from the drop.

üí° Mentor's Tip

As an intraday trader, you mostly want to trade during Phase 2 (Trending Up) and Phase 4 (Trending Down). Phase 1 and 3 are choppy and can hit your stop-losses frequently unless you are very experienced.

üìö Essential Market Vocabulary

To operate professionally, you must know the professional terms.

Liquidity

How easily you can buy or sell shares without impacting the price. High liquidity = Easy to enter/exit (e.g., Reliance, HDFC Bank). Low liquidity = Dangerous (Penny stocks).

Volatility

The range of price movement. High volatility means big swings (good for profit, high risk). Low volatility means small moves (safer, less profit).

Gap Up / Gap Down

When the market opens significantly higher or lower than yesterday's close due to overnight news. This creates a "Gap" on the chart.

Short Selling

Selling a stock you don't own (borrowing from broker) with the intent to buy it back lower. This allows you to profit when markets fall.

üïµÔ∏è‚ôÇÔ∏è Smart Money vs. Retail Money

This is the most important concept for your survival.

  • Smart Money: Banks, FIIs (Foreign Institutional Investors), DIIs (Domestic Institutional Investors). They have billions. They move the market.
  • Retail Money: You, me, and the public. We are small fish. We cannot move the market.

The Strategy: We never fight Smart Money. We identify their footprints (volume, big candles) and we follow them like a remora fish follows a shark. If they buy, we buy. If they sell, we sell.
