ü•ö How Many Eggs in One Basket?

Diversification is the only "free lunch" in investing, but too much of it can lead to average (benchmark) returns. This is called "Diworseification."

üí° The Sweet Spot

For most individual investors, owning 15 to 25 stocks provides the best balance. It's enough to protect you if one company goes to zero, but few enough that your "winners" can still meaningfully drive your total returns.

üìä Position Sizing: Do You Believe?

Not all stocks in your portfolio should be equal. You should weight them based on your conviction and the risk profile.

  • Core Positions (4-7% each): High-quality, stable companies with huge moats. These are your anchors.
  • Support Positions (2-3% each): Growth stocks or cyclical companies that are slightly riskier but have higher potential.
  • Speculative/Satellite (1% or less): High-risk, high-reward bets. Only a small fraction of your portfolio should be here.

üè¢ Sector Diversification

It's not enough to own 20 stocks if they are all in Tech. If the Nasdaq crashes, your whole portfolio will sink regardless of how many companies you own.

‚úÖ Aim for a Mix of Sectors:

  • Growth: Technology, Consumer Discretionary.
  • Defensive: Consumer Staples, Healthcare, Utilities.
  • Cyclical: Financials, Industrials, Energy.

‚öñÔ∏è Rebalancing: Buying Low, Selling High

Rebalancing is the process of bringing your portfolio back to your target weights. If a stock was 5% of your portfolio but grew to 15%, you might be "over-exposed."

Method: Once or twice a year, review your portfolio. Sell a little bit of your winners (which are now overpriced) and add to your laggards (which might now be bargains). This automatically forces you to "sell high and buy low."

‚úÖ Today's Action Steps

üìù Action Step 1: Sector Audit (15 minutes)

If you have a current portfolio (or a watchlist), categorize each ticker by sector. Are you too heavy in one area? If so, identify a high-quality company in a different sector to research next.

üìù Action Step 2: Position Sizing Plan (15 minutes)

Decide on your "Maximum Position Size." For many, this is 10%. This means no single stock can ever represent more than 10% of your total wealth. Having a rule now prevents emotional decisions later.

üîë Key Takeaways

  1. 15-25 stocks is often the ideal range for a focused individual portfolio.
  2. Weightings should reflect your conviction‚Äîdon't treat every stock equally.
  3. Cross-sector diversification protects you from systemic industry crashes.
  4. Rebalancing is a disciplined way to harvest profits and manage risk.
