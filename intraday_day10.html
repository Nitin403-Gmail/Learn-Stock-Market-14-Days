Trading reversals is dangerous. If a stock is falling like a stone, buying it because "it looks cheap" is suicide. We only trade reversals when we see CONFIRMATION that the trend has actually stopped.

Trees don't grow to the sky, and stocks don't fall to zero in a day. Eventually, trends exhaust. We are looking for that moment when the 'Speed' of the trend dies and reverses.

Step 1: Spot the Divergence

Is price making a New High, but RSI is failing to make a new high (below 70)? This means the bulls are tired.

Step 2: Check the Zone

Is this happening at a major Resistance (for Short) or Support (for Buy)? Divergence in the middle of nowhere is useless.

Step 3: The Trigger Candle

Wait for a Reversal Candle (Shooting Star / Hammer / Engulfing). Enter ONLY when the Low/High of that candle is broken.

  • Stop Loss: Strictly above the High (for Short) or below the Low (for Buy) of the reversal candle.
  • Target 1: The 20 EMA line. Price usually magnets back to the average.
  • Target 2: The next Support/Resistance zone.
