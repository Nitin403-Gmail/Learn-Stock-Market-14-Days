There are over 5,000 public companies in the US and thousands more globally. You cannot research them all. A Stock Screener (like Finviz, Screener.in, or TradingView) does the heavy lifting for you by filtering based on the rules we learned in Days 3-5.

ğŸ“‹ The "Antigravity Quality" Screen

Use these settings on your favorite screener to find high-quality companies at fair prices:

âš™ï¸ Filter Criteria:

  • ROE (Return on Equity): Over 15% (A sign of high quality).
  • Debt-to-Equity: Under 0.5 (A sign of safety).
  • Operating Margin: Over 15% (High efficiency/moat).
  • Revenue Growth (last 5 yrs): Over 10% (Growth evidence).
  • Forward P/E: Under 25 (Avoiding extreme overvaluation).

ğŸ” Step-by-Step Walkthrough

  1. Run the Screen: Put the criteria above into the tool. You should go from 5,000 stocks to maybe 30-50.
  2. Scan the Names: Do any of these fall into your Circle of Competence? (Day 2).
  3. Check the 10-Year Trend: Look at the revenue and profit charts. Are they smooth and consistent, or rocky? Smooth charts indicate a predictable business with a moat.
  4. Identify the "Top 3": Choose the three that you find most interesting.

âš¡ The 5-Minute Analysis

Before deep-diving (Day 8), do this for each of your Top 3:

  • Question 1: Does the company make more cash than its reported profit? (Check Cash Flow from Ops vs Net Income).
  • Question 2: Is the number of shares going down? (Indicates buybacks).
  • Question 3: What is the main reason they could go bankrupt? (If no obvious reason, that's a plus).
