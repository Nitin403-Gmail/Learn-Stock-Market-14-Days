üìà What is ORB?

ORB stands for Open Range Breakout. It is the most popular strategy among professional intraday traders because it capitalizes on the massive volatility at the market open (9:15 AM to 10:00 AM).

The logic is simple: The first 15 minutes of the market is a war between buyers and sellers. Once this war settles, the winning side usually drives the trend for the next hour.

‚öôÔ∏è The Setup Rules

  • 1
    Timeframe Selection Set your chart to 15 Minutes. (Beginners should avoid 5 minutes as it's too noisy).
  • 2
    Mark the Range Watch the first candle (9:15 AM to 9:30 AM). Do NOT trade. Once it closes, draw a line at the High and the Low of this candle.
  • 3
    Entry Trigger Buy if a candle CLOSES above the High.
    Sell (Short) if a candle CLOSES below the Low.
  • 4
    Stop Loss (SL) If Buying: SL is below the breakout candle.
    If the range is huge, simple SL is 0.5% or VWAP line.

üö¶ The Filters (When to AVOID)

Not every ORB is valid. Use these filters to save your money.

‚úÖ GOOD SETUP
  • The 15-min range is moderate (not too small, not too big).
  • The breakout happens with Volume expansion.
  • The stock is breaking a major Level (Day 5 concept) along with ORB.
‚ùå BAD SETUP
  • The first candle is massive (>1.5% move already). The move is exhausted.
  • The breakout candle has a huge wick on top (Selling pressure).
  • Price is far away from VWAP. Taking a trade here is risky (rubber band snap-back).
