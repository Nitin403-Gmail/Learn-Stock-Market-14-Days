üß± The Foundation of Technical Analysis

"If I could only teach one concept to a new trader, it would be support and resistance. Master this, and you're 80% of the way there." ‚Äî Professional Floor Trader

Support and resistance levels are price areas where buying or selling pressure is expected to be strong enough to pause or reverse price movement. They represent the collective memory of the market.

Support: Where Buyers Step In

Support is a price level where demand is strong enough to prevent price from falling further. Think of it as a floor.

  • Buyers believe the price is "cheap" and start buying
  • Sellers who shorted want to take profits
  • Previous buyers who missed out see a second chance

Resistance: Where Sellers Step In

Resistance is a price level where supply is strong enough to prevent price from rising further. Think of it as a ceiling.

  • Sellers believe the price is "expensive" and start selling
  • Buyers who bought lower want to take profits
  • People who bought at that level and saw losses want to "get out even"

üí° The Psychology Behind S/R

Support and resistance work because humans have memory. If a stock bounced at $50 three times in the past, traders remember. When it approaches $50 again, buyers anticipate another bounce and buy‚Äîcreating a self-fulfilling prophecy. The more times a level is tested, the more significant it becomes.

üîç 3 Methods to Identify S/R Levels

Method 1: Swing Highs and Swing Lows

The most basic and reliable method. Look for points where price reversed direction.

‚Üê‚îÄ‚îÄ Swing High (Resistance) /\ / \ ‚Üê‚îÄ‚îÄ Swing High (Resistance) / \ /\ / \ / \ / \ / \ \ / \/ ‚Üë Swing Low (Support)

Rule: A swing high requires at least 2 lower candles on each side. A swing low requires at least 2 higher candles on each side.

Method 2: Round Numbers (Psychological Levels)

Humans are drawn to round numbers. $100, $50, $25, $10 are all psychological levels that act as S/R.

  • Major levels: $50, $100, $150, $200...
  • Medium levels: $25, $75, $125...
  • Minor levels: $10, $20, $30...

Method 3: Volume Clusters

Areas where a lot of trading occurred in the past become significant S/R zones. Many platforms show "Volume Profile" which highlights these areas.

üí° Quality Over Quantity

Don't draw 50 lines on your chart. Focus on the most significant 3-5 levels. A good support/resistance level should be:
‚Ä¢ Touched multiple times (2+ touches minimum)
‚Ä¢ Visible on higher timeframes
‚Ä¢ Confluent with other factors (round numbers, moving averages)

üîÑ Role Reversal: The Magic of Flip Zones

One of the most powerful concepts in technical analysis: Old resistance becomes new support, and old support becomes new resistance.

Why Does This Happen?

  1. Trapped traders: People who sold at resistance are now underwater and looking to get out at breakeven
  2. Missed opportunity: Traders who missed the breakout see the pullback as a second chance to buy
  3. New floor established: The breakout proves buyers are stronger, establishing a new floor

üí° The Retest Trade

One of the highest probability setups: Wait for price to break through a level, then wait for the retest of that level before entering. This gives you:
‚Ä¢ Confirmation the breakout is real
‚Ä¢ Better entry price
‚Ä¢ Clearer stop-loss placement

üìä Bounces vs. Breakouts: When to Trade What

Trading the Bounce (Range Trading)

When price is in a range, trade the bounces:

  • Buy when price touches support and shows rejection
  • Sell when price touches resistance and shows rejection
  • Use the opposite level as your target

Trading the Breakout

When price breaks through a level convincingly:

  • Enter in the direction of the breakout
  • Or wait for the retest (safer)
  • Use the breakout level as your new S/R reference

‚ö†Ô∏è Beware of False Breakouts

Not every breakout is real. Many fail and reverse. Signs of a false breakout:

  • Low volume on the breakout candle
  • Quick reversal back below/above the level
  • Breakout against the larger trend
  • Long wicks (rejection candles)

Solution: Wait for the candle to CLOSE beyond the level, or wait for a retest.

üîë Key Takeaways

  1. Support is where buyers are expected to step in; resistance is where sellers emerge
  2. S/R works because of human psychology and market memory
  3. Use swing highs/lows, round numbers, and volume clusters to identify levels
  4. Role reversal (flip zones) creates high-probability trade opportunities
  5. Trade bounces in ranges; trade breakouts when trend is clear
