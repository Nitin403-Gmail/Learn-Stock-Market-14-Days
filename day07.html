üìã Week 1 Concept Review

Day 1: Market Fundamentals & 80/20 Mindset

  • Stock market = marketplace for buying/selling company ownership
  • Prices move based on supply and demand
  • 80% of results come from 20% of concepts
  • Choose your style: Day trading, swing trading, or investing

Day 2: Price Action & Market Structure

  • Price action = studying price movement without indicators
  • 3 market states: Uptrend (HH/HL), Downtrend (LH/LL), Range
  • Trade WITH the trend, not against it
  • Break of Structure signals potential trend change

Day 3: Support & Resistance

  • Support = price floor where buyers step in
  • Resistance = price ceiling where sellers step in
  • Role reversal: Old resistance becomes new support (and vice versa)
  • Trade bounces in ranges, breakouts in trending markets

Day 4: Candlestick Patterns

  • 5 patterns that work: Pin Bar, Engulfing, Inside Bar, Doji, Morning/Evening Star
  • Context is everything‚Äîpatterns only work at key levels
  • Always wait for confirmation
  • Look for confluence (multiple factors aligning)

Day 5: Moving Averages

  • 3 essential MAs: 20 (fast), 50 (medium), 200 (trend)
  • MA order shows trend strength: 20 > 50 > 200 = uptrend
  • MAs act as dynamic support/resistance
  • Trade pullbacks to MAs in trending markets

Day 6: Risk Management

  • Never risk more than 1-2% per trade
  • Position Size = Risk Amount √∑ (Entry - Stop Loss)
  • Place stops at technical levels, not arbitrary distances
  • Only take trades with 1:2 or better risk-reward

üìù Knowledge Check Quiz

Answer these questions to test your understanding. Write your answers before checking.

Question 1: Market Structure

You see a stock making Higher Highs and Higher Lows. What market state is this? What type of trades should you look for?

Show Answer

This is an uptrend. You should only look for buying opportunities (long trades), preferably on pullbacks to support levels or moving averages.

Question 2: Support & Resistance

Price breaks above a resistance level at $100 and then pulls back to test $100 again. What concept is this? How would you trade it?

Show Answer

This is role reversal‚Äîold resistance ($100) becoming new support. You would look to buy on the retest with a stop-loss below $100 and a target at the next resistance level.

Question 3: Candlestick Patterns

You see a hammer candle in the middle of a ranging market. Should you trade it? Why or why not?

Show Answer

No. Candlestick patterns only work at key levels (support, resistance, MAs). A hammer in the middle of a range has no context and is unreliable. Wait for patterns at the range boundaries.

Question 4: Position Sizing

You have a $20,000 account and want to risk 1%. Your entry is $40, and your stop-loss is $38. How many shares can you buy?

Show Answer

Risk amount = $20,000 √ó 1% = $200
Risk per share = $40 - $38 = $2
Position size = $200 √∑ $2 = 100 shares

Question 5: Risk-Reward

Your entry is $50, stop-loss is $48, and target is $56. What is the risk-reward ratio? Is this a good trade?

Show Answer

Risk = $50 - $48 = $2
Reward = $56 - $50 = $6
Risk:Reward = 1:3
Yes, this is a good trade (minimum acceptable is 1:2).

üìä Practical Exercise: Full Trade Analysis

üìù Complete Trade Setup Exercise (40 minutes)

Open TradingView and complete this full analysis for SPY (S&P 500 ETF) on the daily chart:

Step 1: Trend Analysis (5 min)

  • Identify if price is above or below the 200 EMA
  • Note the MA order (which is on top: 20, 50, or 200?)
  • Is the stock in an uptrend, downtrend, or range?
  • Write: "The trend is _______ because _______"

Step 2: Key Levels (10 min)

  • Mark 2-3 major support levels below current price
  • Mark 2-3 major resistance levels above current price
  • Note any round numbers ($400, $450, $500, etc.)
  • Identify any flip zones (role reversals)

Step 3: Entry Opportunity (10 min)

  • Is price at or near a key level?
  • Are there any candlestick patterns forming?
  • Is price pulling back to a moving average?
  • Identify a potential entry price

Step 4: Risk Management (10 min)

  • Where would you place your stop-loss? (Technical level)
  • Where is your target? (Next resistance/support level)
  • Calculate the risk-reward ratio
  • Using a $10,000 account with 1% risk, calculate position size

Step 5: Trade Decision (5 min)

  • How many confluence factors align?
  • Is this a trade you would take? Why or why not?
  • What would need to happen for you to enter?
