Strategy 1: Pullback Entry (Most Reliable)

Wait for a strong trend, then enter on a pullback to support (in uptrend) or resistance (in downtrend).

Setup Requirements:

  1. Clear trend (price above/below all 3 MAs)
  2. Pullback to a key level (20 EMA, 50 EMA, or horizontal S/R)
  3. Reversal candlestick pattern at the level
  4. Confirmation candle in trend direction

Strategy 2: Breakout Entry

Enter when price breaks through a key support/resistance level with momentum.

Setup Requirements:

  1. Price consolidating near a clear S/R level
  2. Strong breakout candle (large body, closes beyond level)
  3. Higher volume on breakout (if available)
  4. OR wait for retest of broken level

Stop-Loss: Below the broken level (now support) + buffer

Strategy 3: Reversal Entry (Higher Risk, Higher Reward)

Enter at the start of a new trend after signs of exhaustion in the old trend.

Setup Requirements:

  1. Extended move in one direction (overextended)
  2. Price at major S/R level
  3. Strong reversal candlestick pattern
  4. Break of structure (change of character)

Stop-Loss: Beyond the extreme of the reversal pattern

Best for: Counter-trend trades at major levels, experienced traders

ğŸ’¡ Entry Selection

For beginners, focus on Strategy 1 (Pullbacks). It has the highest probability and clearest risk management. Only move to breakouts and reversals after you've mastered pullback trading.

ğŸšª Exit Strategies

Exiting Losers (Stop-Loss)

Your stop-loss should be placed at a technical level where your trade idea is invalidated:

  • Pullback trade: Below the swing low of the pullback
  • Breakout trade: Below the broken level
  • Reversal trade: Beyond the extreme of the pattern

Rules:

  1. Set stop BEFORE entering the trade
  2. Use a hard stop (in the system), not a mental stop
  3. Never move stop further away from entry

Exiting Winners (Take Profit)

There are several methods. Choose one and be consistent:

Method 1: Fixed Target

Set target at the next major S/R level. Simple and objective.

Method 2: Risk Multiple

Target = 2x or 3x your stop-loss distance. Easy to calculate.

Method 3: Trailing Stop

Let winners run by moving your stop as price moves in your favor:

  • Trail below the 20 EMA as it rises
  • Trail below each new higher low
  • Move stop to breakeven after 1R profit

Method 4: Scaling Out

Exit your position in pieces:

  • Take 50% off at 1R (first target)
  • Move stop to breakeven
  • Let remaining 50% run with trailing stop

ğŸ’¡ The Best Exit Strategy

For beginners, use Fixed Target at 2R. It's simple, objective, and ensures a positive expectancy. As you gain experience, experiment with scaling out or trailing stops.
