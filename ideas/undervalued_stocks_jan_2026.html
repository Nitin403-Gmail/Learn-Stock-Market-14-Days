Deep Dive Analysis

1. Maithan Alloys ₹1,100

P/E: 6.4 | ROE: 19% | Debt: Zero

Rationale: Maithan Alloys is a niche player in manganese ferro alloys. It trades at a massive discount (single digit PE) despite a pristine balance sheet. The cash on books provides a huge margin of safety (roughly 30-40% of market cap is often cash/investments).

Intrinsic Value Est: Conservative DCF suggests fair value > ₹1,800. Forward P/E re-rating to just 10x implies 50% upside.

2. GHCL ₹550

P/E: 9.1 | ROE: 19.5% | Debt: ~0

Rationale: Following the demerger of its textile business, GHCL is a pure-play Soda Ash manufacturer. It generates massive free cash flow. A P/E of 9 is unjustified for a company with ~20% ROE and market leadership.

Intrinsic Value Est: Graham Number > ₹800. Potential Upside: 45%.

5. Mahanagar Gas (MGL) ₹1,450

P/E: 11.2 | ROE: 18.9% | Debt: Zero

Rationale: A utility monopoly in Mumbai with zero debt and high dividend yield. Regulatory risks regarding gas allocation periodically dampen sentiment, offering entry points. The stock often trades below its long-term median P/E of 14.

Intrinsic Value Est: DCF implies value around ₹1,900-₹2,000 assuming 8% terminal growth.

10. Karur Vysya Bank ₹210

Rationale: One of the best-managed old private sector banks. It has cleaned up its books (GNPA < 1.5%) and improved Net Interest Margins (NIMs). Trading at roughly 1.0-1.2x Price/Book, which is cheap compared to peers like Federal Bank or IDFC First trading at 1.5x-2x.
