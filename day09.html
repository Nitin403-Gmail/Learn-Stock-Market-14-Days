üìã Why You Need a Written Trading Plan

"If you don't have a plan, you're planning to fail." ‚Äî Trading Wisdom

A trading plan is a written document that defines exactly how you will trade. It removes emotion and guesswork from your decisions by giving you a clear set of rules to follow.

Benefits of a Trading Plan

  • Removes emotional decisions: You've already decided what to do in every situation
  • Creates consistency: Every trade follows the same process
  • Enables review: You can analyze if you followed your rules or not
  • Prevents impulsive trades: If it doesn't fit the plan, you don't trade
  • Builds confidence: You know exactly what you're doing and why

üí° The Golden Rule

Never take a trade that doesn't fit your trading plan. It's better to miss 10 opportunities than to take 1 trade that breaks your rules. Consistency beats perfection.

üìù The 7 Components of a Trading Plan

1. Market Focus

Define which markets and instruments you will trade:

  • Which markets? (Stocks, Forex, Crypto, Options)
  • Which specific stocks or pairs? (Large caps, specific sectors)
  • What time frames will you use?

2. Trading Style

Define your trading approach:

  • Day trading, swing trading, or position trading?
  • How many trades per day/week?
  • What hours will you trade?

3. Entry Criteria

Define EXACTLY when you will enter a trade:

  • What setup must be present? (e.g., pullback to 20 EMA at support)
  • What confirmation is needed? (e.g., bullish engulfing candle)
  • How many confluence factors must align?

4. Exit Criteria

Define how you will exit trades:

  • Where will you place your stop-loss?
  • How will you set profit targets?
  • Will you use trailing stops?
  • Under what conditions will you exit early?

5. Risk Management

Define your risk parameters:

  • Maximum risk per trade (1-2%)
  • Maximum daily loss
  • Maximum open positions
  • Minimum risk-reward ratio

6. Routine

Define your daily process:

  • Pre-market preparation
  • During-session monitoring
  • Post-market review

7. Rules & Guidelines

Personal rules to keep you disciplined:

  • When you will NOT trade (news events, tired, emotional)
  • What to do after losses
  • Maximum trades per day

üìù Action Step 2: Create a Pre-Trade Checklist (10 minutes)

  2. ‚òê Does it meet all entry criteria?
